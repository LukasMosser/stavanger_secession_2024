About

Stavanger Secession is an annual international art summit hosting its first edition from June 19-23, 2024.

Preceded by a prologue edition in June 2023, this public, city-wide program invites artists, thinkers, musicians, and filmmakers to explore pressing issues through art installations, discussions, and performances.

Stavanger Secession, inspired by distant avant-garde meeting places like Monte Verità in Switzerland or the Black Mountain College in the USA, unites groundbreaking artists and thinkers globally. Each annual session is thought of as an expedition, gathering minds from various horizons in order to dive into the most crucial contemporary issues. It borrows its ethos from the striking natural beauty of Norway, where the contrast between prolonged darkness and intense light serves as a catalyst for crafting new narratives.

Embracing a historical avant-garde strategy, it raises a fundamental question: How can we disrupt the established order and forge a new reality?

Secession, predating movements or utopias, is the transformative birth act.

Reminiscent of the cinematic and literary journeys depicted in ‘Stalker’ by Andrei Tarkovsky and ‘Mount Analogue’ by René Daumal, it embarks on an exploration where the final destination is uncertain. Its “raison d’être” is to be a forum for speculation and collision of ideas in order to insufflate fear and loathing into the exhausted routines of modern life.
